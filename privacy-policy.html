Sahajanya Multipurpose Co-op. Ltd. (Registration No.7/ARBI dated 18.04.1962) is a cooperative society registered under the Karnataka Co-operative Societies Act. We are committed to protecting the privacy and security of our members' personal information in accordance with applicable data protection laws and regulations.

Last updated: July 16, 2025
This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard the personal information of our members, website visitors, and other individuals who interact with our cooperative society.

1. Interpretation and Definitions

1.1 Interpretation

The words in this Privacy Policy where the initial letter is capitalized have specific meanings defined under the following conditions. These definitions apply whether the terms appear in singular or plural form.

1.2 Definitions

For the purposes of this Privacy Policy:

Member means an individual who has been admitted to membership in Sahajanya Multipurpose Co-op. Ltd. in accordance with its by-laws.

Personal Information means any information that relates to an identified or identifiable individual, including but not limited to name, address, contact details, identification numbers, financial information, and transaction history.

Society or We/Us/Our refers to Sahajanya Multipurpose Co-op. Ltd., registered under the Karnataka Co-operative Societies Act.

Services means all financial and non-financial services provided by the Society to its members, including but not limited to savings accounts, fixed deposits, recurring deposits, and loan facilities.

Website refers to the Sahajanya Multipurpose Co-op. Ltd. website, accessible from sahajanya.co.in.

You/Your refers to the individual accessing or using our Services, or the company, or other legal entity on behalf of which such individual is accessing or using the Services.

KYC means Know Your Customer, the process of verifying the identity of our members as per regulatory requirements.

Regulatory Authorities includes but is not limited to the Registrar of Co-operative Societies, Reserve Bank of India (RBI), National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD), and other relevant government or regulatory bodies.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information We Collect

In the course of providing our Services, we may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Identification Information: Full name, date of birth, gender, photograph, signature, PAN, Aadhaar number, Voter ID, Passport details, or other government-issued identification.
  • Contact Information: Residential address, email address, telephone/mobile numbers, and emergency contact details.
  • Employment Information: Employer details, employee ID, designation, department, and employment history (for CGST and Customs staff).
  • Financial Information: Income details, bank account information, credit history, loan details, transaction history, and tax-related information.
  • KYC Documents: Copies of identity proof, address proof, income proof, and other documents submitted for account opening or loan processing.
  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type, device information, and usage details when you visit our website or use our digital services.

2.2 How We Collect Your Information

We collect information through various means, including:

  • Directly from you: When you apply for membership, open an account, apply for loans, or use our services.
  • Regulatory and Government Authorities: As required by law, regulation, or legal process, including to comply with requests from the Registrar of Co-operative Societies, RBI, NABARD, tax authorities, or other government bodies.
  • Credit Bureaus: To report credit information and obtain credit reports for loan processing and credit assessment.
  • Banks and Financial Institutions: For processing transactions, payment settlements, and other banking operations.
  • Service Providers: Third-party vendors who perform services on our behalf, such as IT service providers, payment processors, auditors, and consultants.
  • Legal and Professional Advisors: Lawyers, auditors, and other professional advisors as needed for our business operations.
  • Other Members: As required by cooperative society laws, certain member information may be shared with other members during general body meetings or as part of the society's records.
  • With Your Consent: We may share your information with other third parties when you have given us your explicit consent to do so.

We require all third parties to respect the security of your personal information and to treat it in accordance with the law. We do not sell your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

5. Data Retention and Security

5.1 Data Retention

We will retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, regulatory, accounting, or reporting requirements. The retention period may extend beyond your relationship with us, as required by applicable laws and regulations, including but not limited to:

  • 10 years for member account records after the account is closed, as per co-operative society regulations.
  • 7 years for financial transaction records as per tax and accounting requirements.
  • Lifetime retention for certain records as required by the Karnataka Co-operative Societies Act and Rules.

When personal information is no longer needed, we will securely destroy or anonymize it in accordance with our data retention and destruction policies.

5.2 Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage. These measures include:

  • Secure storage of physical records in locked cabinets with restricted access.
  • Encryption of electronic data and secure transmission of information.
  • Regular security assessments and updates to our systems.
  • Access controls and authentication mechanisms.
  • Staff training on data protection and confidentiality.

While we strive to protect your personal information, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. Therefore, we cannot guarantee absolute security, and you provide your personal information at your own risk.

6. Your Rights and Choices

As a member or user of our services, you have certain rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access: You can request access to the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction: You can request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete personal information.
  • Withdraw Consent: Where we rely on your consent to process your personal information, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time.
  • Object to Processing: You may object to the processing of your personal information in certain circumstances.
  • Lodge a Complaint: You have the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant data protection authority if you believe your rights have been violated.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details provided in the "Contact Us" section below. We may need to verify your identity before processing your request.

7. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this Privacy Policy.

You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes. Changes to this Privacy Policy are effective when they are posted on this page. Your continued use of our services after such changes constitutes your acceptance of the revised Privacy Policy.
